What do you imagine as your ideal life?

After a short lesson on the mechanics of life, Sadhguru led the group into a guided meditation where he asked a simple question: What does your ideal life look like? This is a question I often find myself thinking about; not so much the question itself, but rather daydreaming about the answer: an abstract ideal life. Never had I been asked to answer such a question with so much depth. I was forced to sit with my thoughts and properly formulate a cohesive design for my life. That’s when it started to feel uncomfortable. As I sat there, being asked this question over and over again, I could only formulate sparks, or fragmented pictures, of what it means to be living my ideal life. Is that how everyone thinks? Can we really imagine the ideal life in one clean trajectory?

Looking into World Models and Model-Based Reinforcement Learning taught me that we would need to imagine many of these trajectories in order to find a locally optimal solution. Perhaps, we are not imagining the ideal life right at this moment, but more so what we believe to be the ideal life.

Irrespective of my internal dialogue, I drudged on. Perhaps we don’t need to know everything; maybe an abstract vision of the future will suffice. Let's assume as humans we are all training and inference models, if a TOLD model in TD-MPC can compress what it has seen into a latent state representation and accurately conduct actions through this model of the world, surely I can as well. That is when my imagination fired on all cylinders! I imagined myself in my lab, writing on the whiteboard and translating those writings into code. Shortly after, I had spoken to a few colleagues about my work, and we pressed on through the day, geeking out about RL and robots. After a long day, I went home to see my partner. We made food, went to the gym, explored the new city we were living in, and went to bed in a really nice apartment with a great view of the city. The overall climate of where I would be living involved lots of sun with a cool breeze that would blow open the window curtains, where food would be freshly caught from the surrounding ocean. I would also spend some time at the beach to play volleyball or soccer. Sometimes I would work outside the lab, in a bustling outdoor café with folks who were simply chatting with one another, no awkward silences invoking the use of a phone, and no borders restricting people from different parts of the world from coming to this café. And then Sadhguru asked, what does your imagined ideal world look like? The liberty to travel across borders like the nomads we once were centuries ago, but now enhanced by technology, where technology does not mandate that we go faster, but rather helps us enjoy the slow, keeping us safe and content.

For the first time in my life, I saw it. We have the potential for this kind of life, each and every one of us. If we keep interacting with like-minded people who ask, What is the ideal life?, surely our imagined trajectories will converge, with compromise, toward an optimal solution. We should never stop asking this question. I look forward to all the imagined trajectories of optimal lives rolling through my imagination on this journey of self-discovery.
